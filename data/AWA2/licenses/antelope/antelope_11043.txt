Zambian Sable Antelope female (Hippotragus niger kirkii)


+---------------+
|  DESCRIPTION  |
+---------------+
The Zambian sable antelope (Hippotragus niger kirkii) is an antelope which inhabits central Angola and western Zambia and is classified as vulnerable. The most handsome of the antelopes, with a deep brown coat and long backward arching horns. Adult males have a deep glossy black coat and with the pronounced curve of their long horns are a very striking animals. 

Female Sable Antelope are chestnut to dark brown darkening as they mature while males are very distinctively black. Both sexes have a white underbelly, white cheeks and a white chin. They have a shaggy mane on the back of their neck. Sable antelope have ringed horns which arch backward
