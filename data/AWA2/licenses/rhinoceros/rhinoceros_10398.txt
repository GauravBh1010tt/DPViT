Rhinoceros - Diceros bicornis


+---------------+
|  DESCRIPTION  |
+---------------+
Rhinoceros (IPA: /raɪˈnɒsərəs/), often colloquially abbreviated rhino, is a name used to group five extant species of odd-toed ungulates in the family Rhinocerotidae. Two of these species are native to Africa and three to southern Asia. Three of the five species—the (Javan, Sumatran and Black Rhinoceros)—are critically endangered.
