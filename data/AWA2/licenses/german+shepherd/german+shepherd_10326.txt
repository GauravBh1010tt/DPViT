The German Shepherd is a relatively new breed of dog, with its origin dating to 1899.  As part of the Herding Group, the German Shepherd is a working dog developed originally for herding and guarding sheep. 

Because of its strength, intelligence and abilities in obedience training, it is often employed in police and military roles around the world.

German Shepherds currently account for about 5% of all dogs registered with the American Kennel Club. Due to its loyal and protective nature, the German Shepherd is one of the most registered of breeds.
